electromagnetism, magnetic-fields, potential, vector-fields
asked by Samyak Marathe on 10:40AM - 14 Aug 21 UTC

The magnetic field however is generally not such a field: in mathematical parlance, we say it is not a conservative field. (As noted in the comments) A notable exception to this is a magnetic dipole, such as a bar magnet, whose magnetic field can be described in terms of a scalar field whose contours run perpendicular to the field lines. This, however, is a special case.
